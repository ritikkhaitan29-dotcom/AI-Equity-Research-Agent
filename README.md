## Overview

Researching a company often requires switching between annual reports, investor presentations, exchange filings, financial websites, earnings calls, press releases and news articles before even deciding whether the business deserves deeper analysis.

The information already exists.

The challenge is identifying what actually matters, connecting insights across multiple sources and evaluating them through a consistent framework.

I built this project to solve that problem.

The AI Equity Research Agent transforms publicly available information into a structured equity research report from a single company name. Rather than replacing investment judgement, the workflow is designed to reduce repetitive research, identify potential red flags early and help investors develop a well-rounded understanding of a business before spending hours on detailed analysis.

Today, whenever I hear someone mention a company as a potential long-term investment, this is the first tool I use before opening an annual report.

## The Solution

Most AI tools perform well when asked individual questions about a company. However, the quality, depth and consistency of the response often depend on how the question is framed.

This project takes a different approach.

Instead of relying on open-ended conversations, it follows a predefined equity research workflow. Every company is analysed using the same structured sequence, ensuring that important aspects of the business are reviewed consistently regardless of the company being analysed.

The workflow evaluates multiple dimensions of a business, including:

- Business model and competitive position
- Industry structure and long-term growth drivers
- Management quality and corporate governance
- Financial performance and earnings quality
- Capital allocation and CAPEX plans
- Valuation using multiple approaches
- Institutional ownership and market expectations
- Business-specific risks and scenario analysis

Rather than simply summarising information, the workflow connects observations across multiple sources to produce an evidence-based investment view.

Several design principles help improve consistency:

- Every company begins with a neutral score and must earn higher ratings through evidence.
- Governance concerns carry significant weight in the overall assessment.
- Missing information is explicitly disclosed instead of being ignored.
- Technical analysis is used only for timing and risk management, not to justify long-term investment decisions.
- Every report concludes by challenging its own investment thesis through scenario analysis and a Devil's Advocate review.

The value of this project lies not in the AI model itself, but in the structured research methodology that guides every analysis.

## Design Philosophy

This project was not built to generate longer stock summaries.

It was built to create a repeatable and disciplined research process.

While designing the workflow, I tried to replicate the way I personally approach a new company. Rather than focusing only on financial statements, the framework combines quantitative analysis with qualitative judgement to develop a balanced view of the business.

Several principles guided the design.

### Capital Preservation First

Long-term investing is as much about avoiding poor businesses as it is about identifying great ones. The framework therefore prioritises capital preservation before return maximisation, with a deliberate focus on identifying governance issues, weak cash generation and other potential red flags.

### Evidence Over Narratives

Management commentary, market sentiment and news flow provide useful context but are not treated as evidence. Conclusions should be supported by publicly available facts wherever possible.

### Consistency Over Complexity

Every company is evaluated using the same research framework. Following a predefined process reduces subjectivity, improves comparability and lowers the risk of overlooking important information.

### Transparency Over Assumptions

Where information is unavailable or uncertain, the workflow highlights the limitation instead of presenting assumptions as facts.

### AI as a Research Assistant

The objective is not to replace investment judgement.

The objective is to reduce repetitive research, improve consistency and allow investors to spend more time analysing businesses rather than gathering information.
## Research Methodology

The workflow follows a fixed research sequence to improve consistency across companies. Rather than relying on open-ended conversations, every report is generated using the same analytical framework.

```text
                Company Name
                      │
                      ▼
        Gather Public Information
                      │
                      ▼
      Business & Industry Analysis
                      │
                      ▼
    Governance & Management Review
                      │
                      ▼
        Financial Statement Analysis
                      │
                      ▼
      Valuation & Market Expectations
                      │
                      ▼
        Risk & Scenario Analysis
                      │
                      ▼
      Structured Investment Conclusion
```

### Stage 1 — Understand the Business

The workflow first establishes what the company does, how it generates revenue, the industry in which it operates and the structural factors that influence long-term performance.

### Stage 2 — Gather Supporting Evidence

Information is consolidated from publicly available sources such as annual reports, investor presentations, exchange filings, earnings calls and other reliable financial disclosures.

Where information is unavailable or inconsistent, the limitation is explicitly disclosed.

### Stage 3 — Evaluate Through a Fixed Framework

Every company is assessed using the same predefined research framework covering:

- Business quality
- Governance
- Financial performance
- Capital allocation
- Valuation
- Ownership
- Investment risks

Using the same framework for every company improves consistency and comparability.

### Stage 4 — Convert Information into Decisions

The workflow translates observations into investment implications using a structured scoring framework.

Rather than rewarding growth alone, the framework gives significant weight to governance quality, earnings quality, cash flow generation and capital allocation.

### Stage 5 — Challenge the Conclusion

Before arriving at a final recommendation, the workflow deliberately challenges its own investment thesis through scenario analysis, risk assessment and a Devil's Advocate review.

This helps reduce confirmation bias and encourages balanced decision-making.

## Current Limitations

While the workflow significantly reduces the effort required to understand a company, it has a few important limitations.

- The analysis is based entirely on publicly available information and does not use proprietary research or paid financial databases.
- AI-generated outputs should be treated as a starting point for research rather than a substitute for independent investment judgement.
- The quality of the report depends on the quality and availability of public disclosures.
- Rapid market developments may occur after the report is generated and should always be independently verified before making investment decisions.

These limitations are intentional reminders that AI is most valuable as a decision-support tool rather than a decision-maker.
